What Are Biases in LLMs

Understanding Encoded Stereotypes in AI Systems

The Problem: Encoded Stereotypes

"Coding stereotype" means that human prejudice is inadvertently hard-coded into artificial intelligence algorithms. Since large-scale language models are trained based on massive data sets retrieved from the Internet, they will absorb historical biases, cultural stereotypes and unequal representation in these data. Artificial intelligence does not identify these text associations as social defects, but regards them as standard statistical models. Therefore, when the model generates responses or evaluates candidate information, it will copy and amplify the stereotypes of these codes, which may lead to unfair results in high-risk applications such as recruitment.

As Gupta et al. (2025) observed, models treat historical statistical associations between gender and profession as standard patterns rather than social defects to be corrected. This means that without deliberate intervention, AI systems will continue to perpetuate and amplify the biases present in their training data.

Reference

Gupta, A., et al. (2025). BiasGym: Fantastic LLM biases and how to find (and remove) them. arXiv preprint arXiv:2508.08855.
