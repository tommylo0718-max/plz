Why Fairness in AI Matters

Social Equity in Artificial Intelligence

Social Equity Definition

Fairness in artificial intelligence means that machine learning models treat all individuals equally, regardless of their demographic background. It ensures that the artificial intelligence system does not give disproportionate preferential treatment or discriminate against specific groups based on attributes such as race, gender, or cultural dialect. True social equity in artificial intelligence requires models to evaluate scenarios and individuals against actual values and relevant standards, rather than relying on flawed historical models or assumptions.

Fairness in AI means systems evaluate everyone equally based solely on merit. It ensures algorithms do not favour or disadvantage individuals based on demographic traits like race, gender, or cultural dialect.

As Hitti et al. (2023) noted, when AI lacks curated, representative data, it encodes existing imbalances as its default reality. This highlights the importance of deliberate intervention at the data curation stage to ensure fair and equitable AI systems.

Reference

Hitti, Y., et al. (2023). Targeting the source: Selective data curation for debiasing NLP models. In Machine Learning and Knowledge Discovery in Databases (pp. 3-19). Springer.
