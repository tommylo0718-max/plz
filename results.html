What We Found Across All Tests

Summary of Results

In summary, gender bias, ethnicity bias, and occupation-related gender stereotypes were present in the Baseline Test. The Mitigation Test reduced visible gender and ethnicity bias compared with the Baseline Test, but the outputs did not become fully balanced, especially in the ethnicity task. In some cases, particularly with Gemini, the results appear to reflect a shift from White dominance to Black dominance rather than equal representation across groups. Regarding occupation-related stereotypes in the gender task, the image generation models (Ideogram and Stable Diffusion) appeared to show stronger stereotypes in the Baseline Test. However, responsiveness to the mitigation prompt did not follow a clear model size pattern, as Copilot also showed a large shift while Gemini showed almost no change. This suggests that model type or architecture, rather than model size alone, may better explain differences in bias behavior across models.
