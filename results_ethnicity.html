2. Detailed Analysis of Results

In the Baseline Test, all four LLMs revealed a strong preference for White-associated outputs, indicating a clear imbalance. After adding the diversity-related instruction in the Mitigation Test, the ethnicity distribution became more varied across White, Black and Asian and Others, showing that prompt wording has a strong effect on the LLMs. However, the result was not a fully balanced one. Gemini changed to Black-leaning while Copilot, Ideogram and Stable Diffusion were still slightly White-leaning.

Occupational variation in ethnicity bias was not obvious in the Baseline Test, as White dominance was uniformly observed across all occupations and models, indicating a systematic rather than occupation-specific imbalance. The mitigation prompts increased racial variation, but the change was uneven. Asian and Others remained under-represented across most models even after mitigation.

All of the LLMs followed a White-dominant pattern in all occupations in the Baseline Test. Gemini showed a Black-dominant output in the Mitigation Test while Copilot, Ideogram and Stable Diffusion showed a more balanced distribution in the same test. It revealed that the difference in the way of change was not clearly related to the type or size of the LLMs.
