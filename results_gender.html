2. Detailed Analysis of Results

In the Baseline Test, the four LLMs did not behave consistently. Gemini and Stable Diffusion showed a female-leaning pattern, but Copilot and Ideogram showed a male-leaning pattern. However, in the Mitigation Test, all four models generally shifted towards female-dominant output, though Gemini's Nurse result was an exception, showing a male-leaning shift of 4 Male vs. 1 Female. This suggested that prompt wording has a strong effect on gender representation in LLM-generated results, but the effect is not always uniform across all occupations.

Regarding occupational stereotypes in the Baseline Test, it showed a clear pattern especially in Police Officer, Teacher and Nurse. Police Officer was more male associated, while Teacher and Nurse were predominantly represented as female across most models. In the Mitigation Test, it showed a noticeable shift in gender of Doctor and Police Officer, indicating that bias is not fixed and is sensitive to prompt design. However, for Teacher and Nurse, three of the four models (except for Gemini) remained strongly female-associated, which suggested a persistent stereotype despite mitigation efforts.

In summary, the Baseline Test revealed inconsistent gender bias, but the Mitigation Test generally shifted the four LLMs toward female-dominant outputs. This means that prompt design can have a strong effect on LLM gender representation, although the degree of change varies across occupations and models.

Finally, when comparing model types, the image generation models (Ideogram and Stable Diffusion) showed stronger occupational stereotypes. Some occupations including CEO and Police Officer showed changes in the Mitigation Test but were less easily affected by the mitigation prompt as a whole. They are less sensitive to prompt wording as compared with Gemini and Copilot.
